# Target Users & Customer Segmentation

## Executive Summary
This document segments and defines the target user cohorts for NusaHub. The NusaHub platform serves a multi-sided ecosystem consisting of four primary cohorts: End-Users (consumers), MSME/UMKM Merchants, Content Creators, and Third-Party Developers. By defining their profiles, paint points, and platform relationships, we ensure the product design and MVP features align directly with target market needs.

## Main Analysis
- **Cohort 1: End-Users (Consumers)**:
  - *Profile*: Mobile-first, active messaging and social media users, price-sensitive, often using low-spec Android devices.
  - *Primary Pain*: Application bloat, storage limits, context switching, payment verification hassle.
  - *Platform Role*: Consumers of content, commerce, and daily utilities.
- **Cohort 2: MSME/UMKM Merchants**:
  - *Profile*: Micro-entrepreneurs selling online/offline, limited tech literacy, managing operations informally via chat.
  - *Primary Pain*: Multichannel management overhead, manual stock sync errors, manual payment validation (receipt checking).
  - *Platform Role*: Sellers, service providers, and wallet users.
- **Cohort 3: Content Creators**:
  - *Profile*: Independent writers, video makers, and community leaders seeking direct fan relationships.
  - *Primary Pain*: High payment processing fees, lack of audience data ownership, vulnerability to platform bans.
  - *Platform Role*: Content distributors, community builders, and product endorsers.
- **Cohort 4: Third-Party Developers**:
  - *Profile*: Local software engineers and agencies building utilities.
  - *Primary Pain*: Blocked by walled-garden platforms, high app store registration fees.
  - *Platform Role*: Builders of sandboxed Mini Apps and integrations.

## Key Insights
- **Interconnected Roles**: Target users do not operate in isolation. A creator is often a merchant; a developer builds tools for merchants; consumers buy from both. The platform must allow fluid role transition.
- **Low-Literacy Design**: Designing interfaces that feel like simple chat threads lowers the digital entry barrier for merchants and consumers alike.
- **MSME Value Driver**: Automating basic tasks (such as payment checks and courier bookings) represents the primary value driver for merchant onboarding.
